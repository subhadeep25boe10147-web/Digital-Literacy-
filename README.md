TASK 5:

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trustworthy entity. This is commonly done through emails, messages, or fake websites.

A typical phishing attack begins with the victim receiving a message that appears to be from a legitimate source, such as a bank or a well-known company. The message usually contains a link and urges the user to take immediate action, such as verifying their account or claiming a reward. When the user clicks the link, they are redirected to a fake website that looks identical to the original. Once the user enters their details, the attacker captures the information.

Students and young individuals are often targeted because they may not always verify sources carefully. The consequences of phishing can include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts.

Raising awareness about phishing and adopting safe online practices can significantly reduce the risk of such attacks.

Prevention Checklist:

Always verify sender email before clicking links
Do not share OTP or passwords with anyone
Use strong and unique passwords
Enable two-factor authentication
Avoid clicking suspicious links
Check website URL before entering details
Use secure networks (avoid public Wi-Fi for transactions)
Regularly update apps and software
UPI Safety: Never approve unknown payment requests
UPI Safety: Verify receiver name before sending money
Report fraud at cybercrime.gov.in
Call 1930 immediately in case of fraud.

While researching phishing attacks, I was surprised by how realistic and convincing fake messages and websites can be. Many phishing attempts closely mimic official communication, making it difficult for users to identify them.

This made me realize the importance of being cautious while interacting online, especially when dealing with sensitive information. One habit I will change is verifying every link and email before taking any action, even if it appears to be from a trusted source.

This task increased my awareness of cyber threats and taught me practical steps to stay safe online. It also highlighted the importance of spreading awareness among others to prevent such incidents.
